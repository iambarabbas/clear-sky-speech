What is Pediatric Speech Therapy?

Pediatric speech therapy helps children develop the communication skills they need to express themselves, connect with others, and succeed in school and social settings. Our licensed speech-language pathologists work with children from age 2 through the teenage years to address a wide range of speech, language, and communication challenges.

At Clear Sky Speech Therapy, we specialize in making therapy fun, engaging, and effective. Whether your child is struggling to pronounce certain sounds, having trouble understanding or using language, or facing communication challenges related to autism or other diagnoses, we're here to help.

Common Conditions We Treat

Articulation Disorders

Difficulty pronouncing sounds correctly (e.g., "wabbit" instead of "rabbit"). We help children produce age-appropriate speech sounds clearly.

Language Delays

Late talkers, limited vocabulary, trouble understanding instructions, or difficulty forming sentences. We build language skills from the ground up.

Childhood Apraxia of Speech

Motor planning difficulty affecting speech. Specialized therapy techniques to help children sequence sounds into words.

Stuttering (Fluency Disorders)

Repetitions, blocks, or prolongations in speech. Gentle, evidence-based strategies to improve fluency and confidence.

Autism Spectrum Disorder

Social communication challenges, echolalia, pragmatic language difficulties. Therapy tailored to each child's unique needs.

Voice Disorders

Hoarseness, vocal nodules, or voice strain from yelling/singing. Vocal hygiene and voice therapy techniques.

Reading & Phonological Disorders

Difficulty with sound awareness, rhyming, or early reading skills. Phonological awareness training to support literacy.

Selective Mutism

Child speaks at home but not at school or in public. Gradual exposure and confidence-building strategies.

Hearing Loss Support

Speech and language development for children with hearing aids or cochlear implants.

What to Expect in Therapy Sessions

First Session: Evaluation

We'll conduct a comprehensive speech-language evaluation to identify your child's strengths and challenges. This includes:

  • Parent interview and case history
  • Standardized and informal assessments
  • Play-based observation
  • Goal setting and treatment plan discussion

Duration: 60-90 minutes

Ongoing Therapy Sessions

Each session is tailored to your child's goals and interests:

  • Warm-up activities and games
  • Targeted practice of therapy goals
  • Play-based activities that keep kids engaged
  • Parent coaching and home practice activities
  • Progress tracking and adjustments

Duration: 30 or 60 minutes, 1-2x per week (varies by need)

Parent Involvement

You're an essential part of the team. At the end of each session, we'll recap what we worked on, show you home activities, and answer questions. Between sessions, you'll have simple strategies to practice at home to reinforce progress.
