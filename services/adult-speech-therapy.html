Comprehensive Adult Speech-Language Services

Communication challenges in adulthood can result from stroke, traumatic brain injury, progressive neurological conditions, voice disorders, or swallowing difficulties. At Clear Sky Speech Therapy, we provide specialized, evidence-based treatment to help adults regain communication abilities, improve vocal health, and restore safe swallowing function.

Our speech-language pathologist holds advanced certifications including VitalStim for dysphagia treatment and has specialized training in adult neurogenic communication disorders.

Conditions We Treat

Stroke Recovery & Aphasia

If you or a loved one has experienced a stroke affecting communication, speech therapy is essential for recovery. We treat all types of aphasia (difficulty with language), apraxia of speech (difficulty planning speech movements), and dysarthria (weakness affecting speech clarity).

Early intervention yields the best outcomes. We work with stroke survivors at all stages of recovery, from acute rehabilitation through long-term maintenance. Learn more about our stroke recovery speech therapy approach.

Voice Disorders & Voice Therapy

Voice problems—chronic hoarseness, vocal fatigue, pitch difficulties, or loss of vocal power—can significantly impact professional and personal life. We provide voice therapy for teachers, speakers, singers, and anyone experiencing voice challenges due to vocal strain, nodules, polyps, paralysis, or age-related changes.

Our approach includes vocal hygiene education, therapeutic voice exercises, and strategies to reduce vocal strain. Read more about voice therapy for professionals.

Cognitive-Communication Disorders

Brain injury, dementia, and other neurological conditions can affect memory, attention, problem-solving, and social communication. Cognitive-communication therapy addresses these challenges through evidence-based exercises and compensatory strategies. Learn more about our cognitive-communication services.

Swallowing Disorders / Dysphagia

Difficulty swallowing (dysphagia) can result from stroke, neurological disease, head and neck cancer treatment, or age-related changes. Our VitalStim-certified speech-language pathologist provides comprehensive dysphagia evaluation and treatment, including neuromuscular electrical stimulation when appropriate.

Safe swallowing is essential for nutrition, hydration, and quality of life. We work closely with physicians and medical teams to develop individualized treatment plans. Explore our swallowing therapy services.

Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)

Following traumatic brain injury, individuals may experience a range of communication challenges including word-finding difficulties, impaired memory, attention deficits, and social communication problems. We provide rehabilitation tailored to each person's specific cognitive and communication needs.

Progressive Neurological Conditions

Parkinson's disease, ALS, multiple sclerosis, and other progressive conditions often affect speech, voice, and swallowing. We offer ongoing therapy to maintain function, teach compensatory strategies, and introduce assistive technology when appropriate.

What to Expect

Comprehensive Evaluation

Your journey begins with a thorough evaluation of your communication and/or swallowing abilities. We assess speech clarity, language skills, voice quality, cognitive-communication, and swallowing function as appropriate to your needs.

Personalized Treatment Plan

Based on your evaluation, we create a customized treatment plan targeting your specific goals—whether returning to work, improving conversation with family, regaining vocal strength, or eating safely again.

Evidence-Based Therapy

We use research-backed treatment approaches including LSVT LOUD for Parkinson's, Constraint-Induced Language Therapy for aphasia, VitalStim for dysphagia, and other proven techniques tailored to your condition.

Flexible Service Delivery

We offer in-person therapy at our Park City location and teletherapy options for appropriate services. Many adult speech therapy services translate well to telehealth, providing convenience and accessibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does adult speech therapy take?

Treatment duration varies by condition and goals. Voice therapy clients often see improvement in 6-12 sessions. Stroke recovery may take several months to a year or longer. We discuss realistic timelines during your evaluation.

Does insurance cover adult speech therapy?

Clear Sky Speech Therapy operates on a private pay model. We provide detailed superbills you can submit to insurance for potential out-of-network reimbursement. Many clients find private pay offers faster access and more personalized care. Learn more about private pay vs insurance speech therapy.

Can therapy be done via teletherapy?

Many adult services work well via teletherapy, including aphasia therapy, voice therapy, and cognitive-communication therapy. Dysphagia therapy typically requires at least an initial in-person evaluation, though follow-up sessions may be conducted via teletherapy depending on your specific needs.

Is it too late to start therapy after my stroke?

It's never too late. While early intervention yields the best outcomes, the brain retains capacity for change throughout life. We work with stroke survivors years post-stroke and help them make meaningful communication gains.
