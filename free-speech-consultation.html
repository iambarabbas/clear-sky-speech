What to Expect During Your Consultation

  • Duration: 15 minutes (sometimes extends to 20-25 if needed)
  • Format: Casual phone conversation—no pressure, no sales pitch
  • Topics: Your concerns, child's or adult's speech/language history, current challenges, goals
  • Outcome: Professional recommendation on whether therapy would help and what type
  • Follow-up: We'll send a brief summary and next steps via email or text

How to Prepare for Your Consultation

You don't need to do much prep, but these tips will help us make the most of your 15 minutes:

  • • Write down your main concerns — What are you noticing? When did it start?
  • • Note any relevant history — Speech delays, ear infections, hearing tests, prior therapy, diagnoses (if any)
  • • Have examples ready — Words they struggle with, sounds they mispronounce, situations where communication breaks down
  • • Think about your goals — What would success look like? What do you hope therapy would help with?
  • • List any questions — Cost, frequency, teletherapy vs. in-person, how long therapy typically takes

Tip: If the consultation is about your child, it's helpful (but not required) to have them nearby so we can briefly hear them speak or observe them if needed.

Consultation FAQs

Is the consultation really free?

Yes! Completely free, no strings attached. We offer this so families can get expert guidance before committing to therapy.

Am I obligated to start therapy after the consultation?

Not at all. The consultation is informational. If therapy makes sense, great—we'll schedule it. If not, we'll point you toward other resources or suggest waiting and monitoring.

Can I do the consultation via video call instead of phone?

Yes! We're flexible. Phone, video call, or even text-based back-and-forth if that's easier. Whatever works best for you.

What if 15 minutes isn't enough time?

We'll extend it! The 15-minute timeframe is a guideline. If you have complex concerns or lots of questions, we'll take the time needed.

Is this consultation for children, adults, or both?

Both! We work with children (speech delays, articulation, stuttering, reading) and adults (stroke recovery, voice issues, swallowing difficulties).

Do you accept insurance?

We specialize in private pay services, which offers more flexibility and personalized care. We can provide a superbill for you to submit to your insurance for potential reimbursement.
